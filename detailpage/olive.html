Data Analytics for Energy Industry Insights

Course Overview: Project management and business analysis are two disciplines that are becoming more strategic for companies in the energy industry. While Project management focuses on the creation of the “product, service, or result” of the project in order to meet its objectives, business analysis aims at understanding the needs of the business, the cost involved, the market, the stakeholders and defining the characteristics of the solution to meeting those needs. The integration of these two disciplines can propel the energy industry towards achieving best project performance, in achieving project objectives and creating business value. The course objectives include:

  • Important fundamentals of project management
  • Delivering projects within timelines and costs assigned, meeting all objectives set for the project.
  • Objective measures and metrics to ascertain project progress and performance
  • Align project objectives to organisational objectives, in turn contributing to organisational growth and development
  • Maintaining good relationships with all concerned stakeholders and development of project charter before the start of a project
  • Project risks and mitigation strategies, management of conflicts, constraints, and other challenges effectively
  • Relationship and differences between project management and business analysis
  • Elements of requirements management and communication and the BA's role in them
  • Necessary competencies and best practices of BAs
  • Enterprise analysis and how to choose appropriate projects.
